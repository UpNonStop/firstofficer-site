The product itself

No. First Officer lives entirely inside WhatsApp, which you already have. There is no app, no dashboard, no login portal, no browser extension. The product is messages.

This is a deliberate choice. Points strategy works best when it shows up in the place you actually pay attention. WhatsApp delivers a snapshot to the same screen you use for everything else, with read receipts and the ability to reply with a question whenever something is unclear. No app fatigue, no second password.

If you do not use WhatsApp, message Ohad on email and we will figure something out.

A snapshot is a personalised summary of your portfolio, sent as a WhatsApp message every Sunday morning. It includes:

  • Total points balance across all programs, with cash equivalent at conservative valuations
  • Recent earning patterns: what category produced the most points last week, which card carried it
  • Three to five named recommendations: a category to redirect, a transfer to consider, a card to apply for or to put away
  • Any program changes that affect you (devaluations, new transfer bonuses, expiring credits)
  • One award booking idea sized to your balance, if relevant

Snapshots are reviewed and edited by Ohad before sending. They are not auto generated. Reserve and Concierge tiers also receive a mid-week pulse if something material changes (a transfer bonus opens, a card category shifts).

Sunday morning: snapshot arrives.

Monday through Friday: nothing scheduled. You message us when you have a question. Common ones: which card to use for an upcoming purchase, whether to transfer points now or wait, whether a card offer is worth taking. Most replies are same business day.

Saturday morning: the UpNonStop dispatch arrives in your inbox. Free, and goes to everyone, member or not. It covers the week in points and miles: notable rate changes, devaluations, transfer bonuses, deals worth knowing about. Members have already seen the most relevant items in their snapshot.

Beyond that, members on Reserve and Concierge get periodic check-ins from Ohad: roughly every 6 to 8 weeks for a portfolio review and a forward-looking strategy session via WhatsApp voice note.

Award booking

$500 per trip is the flat fee for award booking research. Fee is paid once research begins, not at the end. The trip can be one-way, round-trip, or multi-city. Up to four passengers in one record.

What it includes:

  • Routing research across all transferable currencies you hold (Chase UR, Amex MR, Capital One, Citi, Bilt, plus airline-specific balances)
  • Identification of the best partner program for the route
  • Live availability check across primary and alternate dates
  • Step-by-step booking instructions: which transfer to make, when, and what to enter on which website
  • One alternative routing in case the primary becomes unavailable
  • Post-booking support if the airline reschedules or the routing breaks

Concierge tier members get unlimited award booking research per quarter, no per-trip fee.

Standard turnaround: 24 to 72 hours from when we receive your route, dates, and passenger details. Most domestic and one-way international bookings come back inside 48 hours.

Complex multi-leg routes or high-demand windows (peak holiday, business class to premium destinations) can take up to a week. We tell you upfront when the request is going to need extra time.

If we cannot find usable availability after exhausting reasonable options, the fee is refunded in full. We tell you that before charging.

Comparing First Officer

The Points Guy is a content publisher. Free articles, ranked card lists, redemption guides. They earn through affiliate links: when a reader clicks through and is approved for a card, they collect a payment from the issuer. The content is good, but the incentive is to point traffic at cards that pay the most per approval. The card on top of the page is rarely the card that is best for your specific situation.

AwardWallet is a points tracker. It shows what you have, where it is, when it expires. Useful, but it does not tell you what to do with it. AwardWallet is a dashboard. First Officer is a strategist.

First Officer takes no affiliate revenue. The only money we make is your subscription. That changes the entire incentive. The card we recommend is the card that is best for you, even if it pays no commission. The transfer we suggest is the one with the highest cash equivalent for your trip, not the one we are paid to feature.

Yes. Most of what First Officer does, a determined and patient person can do themselves. The information exists publicly across forums, blogs, and program websites. We are not selling secret knowledge.

What we are selling is time and execution. To do this well yourself requires:

  • Tracking 6 to 12 cards across personal and business issuers, including bonus categories, caps, and reset dates
  • Maintaining a current map of merchant category codes for the vendors you actually use (which changes more often than you would think)
  • Watching transfer bonus windows across 5 to 7 transferable currencies
  • Modelling the cash-equivalent value of redemption options at the moment of decision, not in retrospect
  • Knowing which devaluations, fee changes, and program restructurings are coming

If that sounds enjoyable, do it yourself. If it sounds like a part-time job, that is what we are here for.

It does not take any commission, referral fee, or affiliate revenue from any credit card issuer, points program, hotel chain, or airline. Ever. That is the entire reason First Officer exists. Independence is the product.

Revenue comes from one place: subscriber fees. Which means recommendations are based on what is best for your situation, not on what pays the most per click. If the best card for you is one that pays no affiliate commission, that is what we recommend. If the best transfer is to a partner with no referral kickback, that is the move we suggest.

This is the entire pitch. It is also the reason we cannot scale by buying ad inventory the way affiliate sites do. We are betting that being right matters more than being loud.

Trust & data

What we collect: card names you tell us, monthly spend ranges, loyalty programs you participate in, travel goals, and (if you connect Plaid on Select tier or above) read-only transaction data.

What we do not collect: card numbers, account credentials, full account numbers, balances, login passwords, or any financial information that could be used to authenticate as you. None of it touches our systems.

Where it is stored: Supabase, US-based, encrypted at rest. Conversation history on WhatsApp is retained to maintain context for your portfolio. We do not sell, share, repurpose, or train models on your data.

Deletion: message Ohad to delete your account, or use the Do Not Sell My Info link in the footer. Your data is removed within 30 days. The Privacy Policy linked in the footer has the full breakdown.
