The mistakes

What Law Firms Get Wrong

These aren't obvious. Nobody in the law firms industry talks about points optimization. Here's what we see over and over.

01

Paying for Westlaw and LexisNexis on a basic firm card

Legal research platforms may code as professional services or software depending on how they process payments. If they code as software, the the right category card earns 4x. Even at the base 2x on catch-all card, you're doubling your return on $3K+/month that's currently earning 1x.

The math

$3K/month research at 3x avg = 108K pts/year vs 36K at 1x.

02

Not maximizing office supply store purchases at 5x

The a gas and office bonus card earns 5 points per dollar at office supply stores. Staples, Office Depot. Paper, toner, filing supplies, even tech accessories from these stores earn at the highest rate available on any business card.

The math

$1.5K/month office supplies at 5x = 90K pts/year. At 1x = 18K. That's 5x the return.

03

Running marketing spend on the firm's generic card

Google Ads, Avvo, FindLaw, and social media marketing all earn 3x on the right business card or 4x on the right card. For firms spending $4K+/month on client acquisition, this is the highest-impact card swap you can make.

The math

$4K/month ads at 3.5x avg = 168K pts/year vs 48K at 1x.

04

Booking travel through the firm without routing to a travel-bonus card

Court appearances, depositions, and bar conferences often involve flights, hotels, and car rentals. All earn 3x on the right business card. A firm booking $3K/month in travel on a 1x card is leaving 72,000 points per year behind.

The math

$3K/month travel at 3x = 108K pts/year. Worth $1,000 to $2,000.
