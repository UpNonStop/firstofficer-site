The mistakes

What Marketing & Creative Agencies Get Wrong

These aren't obvious. Nobody in the marketing & creative agencies industry talks about points optimization. Here's what we see over and over.

01

Running client ad spend on a flat-rate agency card

If your agency manages $15K/month in Google and Meta ad spend on behalf of clients, that earns 3x on the right business card or 4x on the right card. At 1x, you're leaving 360,000 to 540,000 points per year behind. That's $3,600 to $10,000 in travel.

The math

$15K/month ads at 3.5x = 630K pts/year vs 180K at 1x. Gap: 450K pts.

02

Paying Adobe Creative Cloud and project tools on a personal card

Adobe, Figma, Canva, Monday.com, Slack, and other software subscriptions may code as software on Amex, earning 4x. Agencies stack up $2K+/month in tools easily.

The math

$2K/month software at 4x = 96K pts/year vs 24K at 1x.

03

Booking client travel and production trips on a generic card

Flights, hotels, car rentals, and production travel all earn 3x on the right business card. Agency teams that travel regularly for clients, conferences, or shoots compound significant value.

The math

$3K/month travel at 3x = 108K pts/year.

04

Not recognizing your agency has the best bonus-category profile of any industry

Almost everything an agency spends money on hits a bonus category: ads (3x-4x), software (4x), travel (3x), phone/internet (3x-5x). The only low-earning category is freelancer payments and rent. A 4-card stack captures 2.2 to 2.8x on average.

The math

$28K/month at 2.5x avg = 840K pts/year. Worth $8,400 to $16,800 in travel.
